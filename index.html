About Me

I have a background in computational plasma physics, where I specialize in running large-scale simulations on some of the most powerful high-performance computing (HPC) systems across Europe. My research merges theoretical concepts with advanced computing techniques to address critical questions in plasma physics, particularly focusing on the generation of magnetic fields at small scales. This includes studying key phenomena like the Weibel/Current Filamentation Instability and the Biermann Battery, which are vital for understanding both lab-based plasma experiments and astrophysical processes.

As an HPC Application Engineer, my work centers on:

  • Developing parallel algorithms for scientific computing
  • Optimizing algorithms for GPU efficiency
  • Creating educational resources to empower researchers in parallel computing and GPU utilization
The goal is to make computing more efficient and help researchers get better results faster.

I lead the Scalable Parallel Astrophysical Codes for Exascale (SPACE) Center of Excellence (CoE) at CINECA, which focuses on re-engineering key astrophysical codes to tackle new computational challenges through innovative programming paradigms and software solutions.
